ABOUT

Pratyarth is an annual symposium on seminal research in bioinformatics organised by COMBIGS, the Computational Biology Group at SASTRA Deemed University. This year, our focus is on the use of machine learning techniques, tools and practices in bioinformatics for better diagnostics and digital therapeutics.

To know more about COMBIGS, find out below.



SPEAKERS

Dr. Ashwin Dalal

CDFD, Hyderabad


Finding novel genes for Single gene disorders



Dr. Ashwin Dalal is the Head of Diagnostics at the Centre for DNA Fingerprinting and Diagnostics, Hyderabad. His current research lies in the identification of novel genes for single gene disorders, molecular genetic analysis for lysosomal storage disorders and characterization of rearrangement of chromosomes in human genetic disorders. His interests are neurogenetics and fetal medicine.

Prof. Sitabhra Sinha

IMSc, Chennai


Cancer "Module"-omics: Analyzing the network of cancer diseases and genes reveals "movers" and "shakers" of the disease


Dr. Sitabhra Sinha is a Professor of the physics group at the Institute of Mathematical Sciences as well as an adjunct faculty of the National Institute of Advanced Studies, Bangalore. His current areas of research are broadly under complex systems, nonlinear dynamics and computational biophysics. He has also authored three books in physics and has a wide array of journal publications under his name.

Dr. Rohit Gupta

CIO, MEDGENOME, Bengaluru


Changing landscape of healthcare and personalized medicine with advances in big data and machine learning technologies



Dr. Rohit Gupta is the Chief Information Officer (CIO) at MEDGENOME, Bengaluru, India and an Adjunct Faculty at Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology Delhi (IITD) and IIT-M. He is highly skilled at data mining and computational biology techniques with a fifteen- year experience in applying data driven models to a myriad of domains. His primary research area is genomic data analysis.

Dr. Sabarinathan. R

NCBS, Bengaluru


Deciphering the mutational processes and driver mutations in cancer genomes



Dr. Sabarinathan Radhakrishnan is a faculty member at the National Centre for Biological Sciences. His group aims to apply computational and functional genomics approaches to understand the genetic and molecular basis of human genetic diseases, with a special focus on cancer. His work analyses large-scale cancer genomics datasets obtained from the Indian population, including whole genome/exome and transcriptome data.

Prof. Sudha Natarajan

SASTRA Deemed University, Thanjavur


Convolutional Neural Networks


Dr. Sudha Natarajan is a Professor at the School of Computing, SASTRA Deemed University, Thanjavur and a former faculty member for a decade and half at IITM and Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. She possesses five years of experience in industrial R&D at Tata Elxsi. Her current research areas of interest include machine vision, VLSI, embedded systems and machine learning.
